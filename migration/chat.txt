where do i find execution role arn
You don't find it — it doesn't exist yet. Someone with IAM permissions in the boss's account needs to create it first, then the ARN appears once it exists. This was a deliberate design choice from your original brief ("IAM roles and policies are out of scope for automation... don't write code that creates roles") — so this is the one piece that has to be a manual, human step, not something I do for you even with credentials.

How to create it (AWS Console, ~5 minutes)
Log into the boss's AWS account, go to IAM → Roles → Create role
Trusted entity type: AWS service → Lambda
Permissions: skip attaching managed policies for now (easier to add an inline policy after), but do attach AWSLambdaBasicExecutionRole (search for it in the list) — that's the CloudWatch Logs permission every Lambda needs
Name it something like ai-marketing-hub-lambda-execution-role, create it
Open the new role, go to Add permissions → Create inline policy, and add the rest of what's listed in SETUP_GUIDE.md Step 2:
DynamoDB actions (GetItem, PutItem, UpdateItem, DeleteItem, Query, Scan) on the 14 tables (and their indexes)
s3:GetObject, s3:PutObject on the new bucket
bedrock:InvokeModel, bedrock:Converse (scoped to the model ARNs in us-east-1 and us-west-2)
bedrock:ListFoundationModels (us-east-2)
scheduler:CreateSchedule/UpdateSchedule/DeleteSchedule/GetSchedule
iam:PassRole on the second small EventBridge-invoke role (also needs manual creation — same guide section)
lambda:InvokeFunction on generate-marketing-asset and social-publish-handler-new's ARNs
Once saved, click into the role — the ARN is right at the top of the role's summary page, format:

arn:aws:iam::<his-account-id>:role/ai-marketing-hub-lambda-execution-role
